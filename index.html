History of Grey's Anatomy

Grey's Anatomy first premiered in 2005 and quickly became one of the most popular medical dramas on television. Created by Shonda Rhimes, the show follows the lives of surgical interns, residents, and attendings as they navigate the challenges of both their professional and personal lives. The series is named after the classic medical textbook Gray's Anatomy, which is a fundamental resource for medical students.

Grey's Anatomy is the longest-running American medical drama. Throughout the years, the cast and crew have won or been nominated for numerous awards, including Emmys, Golden Globes, and SAG Awards. The current show runner is Meg Marinis. It is broadcast on ABC on Thursdays, 8/7c. Despite featuring several main characters, the central and titular character is Dr. Meredith Grey, who also functions as the show's primary narrator.

Fun Facts About Grey's Anatomy

Here are some interesting fun facts about the show:

  • The show has aired for over 21 seasons and counting. Season 22 is coming soon.
  • Sandra Oh (Cristina Yang) won a Golden Globe for her role.
  • The series is filmed in Los Angeles, not Seattle.
  • Many real surgeons consult on the show for accuracy.
  • The iconic theme songs are "How to Save A Life" by The Fray & "Chasing Cars" by Snow Patrol
